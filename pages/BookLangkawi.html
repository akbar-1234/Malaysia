Langkawi Cable Car | The Highest Cable Car in Malaysia

Langkawi Cable Car, also known as SkyCab, is the highest cable car in Malaysia, offers a 2.2km journey up the eastern cliffs of Gunung Machinchang. At 42 degrees, it has one of the steepest cable car inclines globally. Opened in 2003, it transports passengers to the peak at a staggering 708 meters above sea level, boasting views of the Langkawi archipelago, and even glimpses of Southern Thailand on clear days.

This award-winning engineering feat is more than just a scenic ride; it's the gateway to Langkawi's premier geo-theme park, Panorama Langkawi, where you can experience the thrill of the SkyBridge, indulge in other activities, and create memories that will last a lifetime.

Read on to learn about the best Langkawi Cable ticket options, what to expect, and everything you need to plan your unforgettable Langkawi Cable Car adventure.

Your Langkawi Cable Car ticket options explained

Standard tickets

  • Enjoy access to a standard gondola SkyCab and ascend 708 meters into the sky
  • Also get access to SkyTrail, 3D Art Langkawi, SkyRex, and SkyDome

Express lane

  • Skip the lines and enjoy priority entry to your Langkawi cable car gondola
  • Upgrade & enjoy a fun-filled day at Panorama Langkawi with access to SkyBridge, SkyGlide, and Eagle's Nest Skywalk

Premium experience

  • Take your Langkawi cable car experience to a luxe level with 360° views from a glass-bottomed gondola.
  • You can also upgrade to get a gondola all to yourself (max 3 persons)
  • Don’t miss 3D Art Langkawi, SkyRex, SkyDome, and even Machinchang Petland Langkawi depending on your choice of ticket

Complete Panorama Langkawi package

  • Go beyond just the cable car ride and experience all that Panorama Langkawi has to offer
  • Spend the day at SkyTrail, 3D Art Langkawi, SkyRex, and SkyDome after your SkyCab ride, and even upgrade to SkyGlide

Guided tour

  • Know Langkawi inside out through an expert local guide
  • In addition to your cable car ride, get hotel pickups and drop-offs, visit the Oriental, and Langkawi Waterfall, and admission to other attractions including SkyBridge

Combo

  • Combine your cable car adventure with a stop at the Langkawi Selfie Museum for playful photo sessions or explore Langkawi Underwater World to see marine life up close
  • Save money and time and get direct entry to SkyDome, SkyRex, & 3D Art Langkawi as well

General timings: 9am to 6:30pm

Duration: The cable car ride itself takes approximately 15-20 minutes. However, your total experience, including browsing exhibits, enjoying the views, and potentially visiting other attractions like the Skybridge, can take anywhere from 1 to 2 hours

Best time to go:

  • Weekday mornings: Avoid the weekend crowds and enjoy shorter queues by visiting near opening hours
  • Clear days: Maximize your views and avoid potential discomfort from strong winds
  • Shoulder seasons (March to May & September to November): Experience pleasant weather and potentially fewer crowds compared to peak tourist seasons (December to February)

Address: Jalan Telaga Tujuh, 07000 Langkawi, Kedah, Malaysia. Find on Maps

As Langkawi is an island, reaching the cable car requires traveling by air or ferry. Here's a quick guide to distances and approximate travel times from popular starting points:

  • Kuala Lumpur (KL): Distance: 506km (approx). Travel time: Flight: 1 hour 45 minutes (fastest option), train & ferry: 8-10 hours (combined travel time)
  • Penang: Distance: 110km (approx). Travel time: Ferry: 30 minutes to 1 hour, flight: 30 minutes (fastest option)
  • Kuala Perlis: Distance: 70km (approx). Travel time: Ferry: 1.5 hours
  • Kuala Kedah: Distance: 130km (approx). Travel time: Ferry: 2 hours
  • Tamalang: Distance: 90km (approx). Travel Time: Ferry: 1.75 hours

Remember

  • Flying directly to Langkawi International Airport might be the quickest option for many travelers
  • Ferry connections offer a scenic journey but require additional travel time
  • Arrival: Arrive at least 30 minutes before your designated ride time for security checks and boarding procedures
  • Luggage: Large luggage and bulky items are not permitted on the cable car. Lockers are available for storing luggage at an additional cost
  • Safety: Follow all safety instructions provided by the staff. Remain seated throughout the ride and keep your arms and belongings inside the gondola at all times
  • Food and beverages: Bringing outside food and beverages onto the cable car is not permitted. However, there are restaurants and cafes available at the top station (Sky Complex) where you can purchase refreshments
  • Smoking: Smoking is strictly prohibited inside the cable car stations and gondolas. Pets: Pets are not allowed on the cable car or within the Langkawi Cable Car complex

While food and beverages cannot be brought onto the cable car, you have several dining options at the Langkawi Cable Car complex:

  • Sky Bistro: Enjoy a delicious meal with sky-high views of the rainforest and surrounding islands
  • Eagle Cafe: Grab a quick bite or light refreshments at this casual cafe
  • Oriental Village Restaurants: Explore various restaurants offering diverse cuisines within the Oriental Village complex
  • Oriental Village: Explore a replica of a traditional Malaysian village with shops, restaurants, cultural performances, and themed gardens
  • Langkawi Sky Bridge: Take a walk on the thrilling 125-meter-long suspension bridge offering breathtaking panoramic views
  • Langkawi Sky Glide: Enjoy a scenic chairlift ride through the rainforest canopy
  • Eagle Nest: Ascend to the highest viewing platform on Langkawi for 360-degree views
  • Teluk Burau Beach: Relax on the sandy beach, popular for swimming, sunbathing, and water sports activities
